# Day 1 Mini Lab – Phishing Analysis

## Scenario
A user receives an email claiming their bank account will be locked unless they click a link immediately.

## Step 1: Identify Red Flags
- Urgency ("act now")
- Threat (account will be locked)
- Unknown or suspicious link
- Possible spoofed sender

## Step 2: What Type of Attack Is This?
Phishing attack (social engineering)

## Step 3: What Should Be Done?
- Do not click the link
- Report the email to IT/security team
- Delete the email
- Verify directly with the bank through official channels

## Step 4: Why This Works
Attackers exploit fear and urgency to trick users into acting quickly without thinking.

## Step 5: Analyst Thinking
If I were a SOC analyst:
- I would check email logs
- I would verify sender domain
- I would look for similar emails across the organization
- I would alert other users if needed

## Key Takeaway
Many attacks succeed not because systems are weak, but because human behavior is exploited.
